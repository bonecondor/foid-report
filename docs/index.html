FIFTEENTH POST

July 21, 2026

I wrote this one on the train back to New York, full of yearning. There's a good heap of narratively self-romanticizing happening. I’m listening to music from my youth. Something about that nightmare knocked loose some Oldness in me. This report is all over the place. I didn’t take my meds on a weekday. It happens.

I woke up to a beautiful sight: the church across the street was blowing thousands of bubbles into the air. It was whimsical. I love bubbles, I love silly little things like this. I love churches. They’re beautiful, most of the time. They can be weird, they can be totally ugly on the outside, but something about wide open spaces really gets me. More on that in a second.

I think churches played a huge role in society, and while I don’t align with them on a religious level, I do think they served as an important Schelling point for otherwise drifting folks to come together. We don’t have a lot of those spaces left (wow, so novel, writing about the lack of third spaces) but I do think the erosion of these kinds of places of worship and belonging is probably a net negative to the psyche.

Now more than ever, people need something larger than themselves. To latch on to? To cling to? They need to hold it closely, dearly, tightly.

We need things to believe in, we need stories, we need mythologies, we need characters that are larger than life. We need protagonists and main characters. People like to make fun of them (often fairly), but without them what would we have?

This is similar to how I feel about Artists and their role in society. If they aren’t allowed to be insane, who is? Businessmen?

To go back to the open spaces thing: I am fascinated architecturally by negative space, by spaces that leave room for Absence. Unsuprisingly I’m a fan of the liminal. The in-between. I’m moved by my own sense of awe conjured in certain containers. It’s incredible that we can manipulate and curate environments to elicit feelings; even more than that, we can engineer reactions in humans due to their built environments.

I've been ruminating on Axel Vervoordt's philosophy. His main idea is that by focusing on vast proportions and natural light, you can feel a spark of divinity. There are cathedrals everywhere, for those with eyes to see.

Vervoordt’s designs draw heavily from mountain temples and ancient eastern monasteries, where a lack of distraction fosters closeness to God. Kanye West’s house was designed such that the proportions are the decoration.

This can be true of almost anywhere, if only you have the courage to look. I love applying this lens in train stations, in places that are meant to demarcate ferrying you from one place to another. I imagine the entrance to the Underworld is much like this, as is the very banks of the river Styx. I imagine all sorts of bridges and branches and petals of paper and metal, twisting and arcing. I am forever fascinated by impossibility in topology.

After I have written the above, it will surprise you that behemoth structures freak me out. Very tall windmills elicit a strange feeling in my belly that isn’t too far removed from fear. It’s not some perversion of a fear of heights. I’m not afraid of heights. I’m not afraid of many things.

In fact, I am afraid of two things: spiders and human error.

The second one deserves more explanation than the first. When I am somewhere where I must trust that things have been done correctly, rules have been followed, and maintenance has been performed to spec, I am often scared. I felt this most acutely on a cable car up Sugarloaf in Rio… it isn’t so much that the height scared me, it’s trusting that the mechanics have properly serviced the cables, that the suspension has been tested accurately, and that no one cut corners while designing it. My only salve for that ride (while spectacular) was the idea that if a cable car full of Americans fell to their death in Brazil, it would be an international incident.

Not that it was much comfort. They can put that on my headstone, I guess.

Spiders just give me a gut reaction. It’s something somatic, something deep down that unsettles me. The creepypastas about this have always struck me as maybe something close to true — that is, the idea that there’s some Creature of the Past that spiders and other associated bugs remind us of, and it’s something encoded deep within our blood. Some sort of primal, ancient fears.

To switch gears entirely: I saw many beautiful, interesting things today. The bubbles were a good omen. We went to the Mall, and I saw all sorts of inspiring technology: Edison’s lightbulb, a bit of ENIAC, high ceilings with arching and intricately patterned inlays, sparkling in the sun.

We saw gold Buddhas, Ted Kaczynski’s handcuffs, a postcard with moon dust on it (the astronaut stamped it in space), a piece of mail from the Hindenburg, and a paper with headlines from the day after Black Monday. It was mind-bending, and renewed my faith in both America and American desire to prosper.

On the train home, I took a call about a spectacular idea that troubled me. Not because of the novelty of the idea, but because my eyes are opened to some hard to swallow truths. The idea is wonderful. Huge. Paradigm shifting. The problem is… I am concerned the Founder does not have the Juice. He doesn’t seem the type to be inspired by Greatness. He doesn’t seem to have the level of conviction I would desire out of someone who could change the world.

We’re (I’m being narcissistic, but I don’t fucking care, it’s my blog) a unique people, determined to the point of hard-headedness. We’re insane, but you have to be if you want to do anything great.

Visiting a Haunted Place of Great Men was good for me. I am heading home feeling renewed, if not a little disjointed and sort of shaken out of my lately more-defined Body.

Speaking of disjointed… an update to the Group Chat Extended Universe: the chat where I stuck everyone else in a blender has congealed a little more. I tried to steer it a little, tried to enact Cybernetics, but it turns out I’m a baby in that field (and in most. I am such a confident novice at Life!)

While I think my criticism was well-received, the end state is not something I could have accounted for. Most of the “Interesting” new people, or I guess “Powerful” or “higher class” people have not spoken. Though I do think it’s curious who has hung around. Participation has dwindled in quality, though liquidity remains deep at most hours.

What has been interesting though, is the new little connections and side-chats that are blossoming around it. There’s something curious about the way these ecosystems work. I haven’t figured out the metaphor for it yet. It turns out that too many beautiful women in a chat makes others scared, but if you scare the beautiful women, you end up with chudchat.

I think this chat has avoided that fate so far, but it’s not clear it’ll survive this next phase of life. I think of these chats less like venues and more like creatures these days. I think chat liquidity is a correct theory, a good lens to use, but it’s not necessarily the best description. These things seem to move like wild animals, transporting meaning across the globe instantaneously, but much more like a swarm of flies (or if I’m being nice, starlings) than a clean “reflexivity” behavior.

A friend (confidant? Underling? That’s horrible, I don’t mean it like that) told me some slightly startling information that I don’t know how to digest just yet. I am clumsy with backchanneling, clumsy with understanding, and burnt by guessing. Burnt harder by projection, and desiring plain language. I think (No, I know) I resent being projected Upon and then left to pick up the pieces myself, but I do really desire reconciliation with most people. There are only a few people I’ve deleted that I actually meant to, and don’t want to disturb the bones of.

What do you think, reader. Shall I kick a hornet’s nest? Shall I startle a wild animal?

Is silence cruelty or relief?

Today was a really lovely (if not sweltering) day in our Nation’s Capital. We took the train down on Saturday, which ended up being quite a strange experience.

Saturday morning I woke up early, regretfully, and packed a bag. I took it with me to a cafe to meet an up-and-coming internet celebrity and my friend who made me the custom hat. We chatted, and the skies opened up on us and dumped gallons of water.

Emergency flash flood warnings lit up my phone, lightning striking every few moments, and swells of thunder were audible inside our comfortable, cozy little brick cafe in midtown. It was like the sky itself was throwing a tantrum, or sobbing hysterically. If the Ancient Greeks were consulted, they would have assumed Zeus was heartbroken or very angry. It’s unclear which.

Walking the 700 or so feet to the train station was treacherous, and I was soaked. I got some snacks in the too-cold air of Penn Station, and boarded the NE Regional right on time.

It’s incredible to me the sheer infrastructural buildup of the Northeast. The economic engine of opportunity that relatively small land area has produced is incredible. There is nothing like it anywhere else I’ve been. It’s almost as if the land itself is blessed, abundant with opportunity.

I marveled at the fact that this train had started in Boston, but would be in Newport News, Virginia, by nightfall… though I found the name Newport News ridiculous. It’s literally the name of the news stands and snack spots in airports! What do you mean they have an entire town dedicated to that?

Anyway, the train was doing okay until we made it to about Baltimore. After we departed, we suddenly stopped. We remained stopped for a while, and then our very chatty train conductor, in his light Jamaican patois, told us we would actually have to travel backwards for a bit.

Apparently, the biblical storms and flash flooding had washed away some signals, and so we couldn’t reliably proceed forward. Without the signals, no one would know exactly where an individual train was, and this is dangerous, for obvious reasons.

We started maybe 15 miles from DC, but the train went backwards nearly all the way to Baltimore. I nodded off, and when I woke up, we had finally started moving in the correct direction again.

Our train was meant to arrive into WAS at 4:41. We finally arrived at 6:25. The Jamaican conductor, tone noticeably more harried, mentioned four or five times that we would be stopping long enough to swap engines, have a smoke, and then a new conductor would continue the journey. He sounded like he needed that smoke quite badly.

From the moment I stepped off the train I felt very disturbed. I cannot explain it, but there is a presence here. I’m not sure it likes me, either. DC has always been a weird place for me. It’s full of ghosts, full of coincidence, and frankly I think the whole thing is haunted.

I don’t want to talk about the things that occurred the first time I came here. It was more than a few lifetimes ago. No one from that life still remembers me. I hope.

That’s not true. I hope I haunt them, angrily, with hate and spirit and fight. I hope I rattle chains when they’re trying to sleep. I hope —

Anyway.

On Sunday, my mom and I got new tattoos. My mom loves tattoos. They’re something like stories to her, somewhere in between battle scars, paint, and memorials. She loves to collect. She has an entire arm she’s working on as a “passport sleeve”, where she gets a different tattoo everywhere she visits. I think this concept is really neat. She borrowed it from me, sort of, with the tattoo I got in Australia.

Wrist smarting but newly full of symbolism, we went on a 2 hour tour in a Model T. It was blazing hot, and by the end, I had wilted. I’m like a flower, but one that needs partial shade.

The tour was full of schizophrenia-inducing facts about America, and how it simply must have been willed into existence by a higher force. America is blessed beyond all belief, and the people that brought it into existence are (were) insane, brave men. I increasingly believe there is no such thing as coincidence, and that synchronicities are clues. Into what? That’s a good question… for another time.

I had a nightmare that night. I woke myself up with a half choked gurgle of a scream. It’s been years since I’ve done that. In the nightmare, someone called me a name no one’s called me since years before DC. I’ve spent all day today with the strangest urge that I should leave here immediately. It feels like a bolt of lightning in my chest. It’s like something is insistent I don’t belong here, that I shouldn’t be here, and that I should go home. It’s very unsettling.

This is a very negative report, despite me having a largely really nice time. It’s wonderful to see my parents! Today I saw some really beautiful paintings, and went to the national botanical garden. I love getting to see Monet in real life: nothing he does measures up in prints at all. The depth is lost, emotion flattened. Playing with distance in real life, walking away, looking back, and feeling the scenes are my favorite way of enjoying Monet.

I also saw some other really spectacular paintings, some full of playful light, others thick with solemn contemplation, and almost all heavy with meaning. It’s truly wonderful that basically everything on the mall is free. Civic society needs more of this.

Speaking of civic society. I am up to something large. I wasn’t going to take work calls while I was here, but I made an allowance for two specific things: capital, and other grand plans. If I pull this off right, it might earn me a wikipedia page. I’m not afraid of admitting that that’s a goal of mine. It’s just true. I figure I can maybe pull that off this year, with a few more tricks up my sleeve and the back half of the year still to come. I will need artists, but they will come. The spectacle demands that I will need reporters, influencers, spectators. Witnesses. I have no doubt that they will come. They always do. It doesn’t stop me from being terrified every time, but the only way forward is through, and so.

There is a new Fringe Report, and I admit, it is a freaky one. I have been talking to teams making biological chips, and this sent me down a rabbit hole into quite strange futures. I wonder which one will come to pass.

I wonder often if you read this. I wonder if you read the fringe.report. I wonder how haunted you are. I wonder, and am full of wonder, for all of the futures that will come true.

The whole time they sat across from us, they would comfortably rest their heads on each other, hold hands, and do little dances. It’s really interesting to watch a word bloom.

I don’t mean like, you invent a new word, and then watch it catch on. I mean that it’s really neat to watch someone develop a language, or reach for a word that means something slightly different, and rotate that into being something everyone else starts using.

I wonder often how these things work. I think there are multiple kinds of people that are language benders, and one such class is the amplifier. They don’t necessarily create the word, or use it for the first time in a novel way, but they are the one that give it attention. They incorporate these things into their language, and then they broadcast it outward with something like embedded meaning and spread power, and then people start picking it up.

I’ve noticed that this happens with me and some of my friends (and things that are more complicated than friends) often. It’s especially apparent on twitter that these kinds of people like to group together, and play with the shape of words. They’re word rotating, so to speak.

I’ve had quite the productive day today. Husband is out of town, and I was exhausted so much (see previous posts) that I went and picked up three different nicotine vapes to try and help me get through. Spoiler alert: I am smoking them all like a little raccoon freak. I am totally feral. I am eating chicken out of the fridge with my bare hands at 1:30am. I am sleeping in three day old clothes. That, and like, It’s 95 degrees today in New York.

I love watching plants toss and turn, a little beaten down by something that will help them flourish dramatically, in the right proportions. I’m on this new medication that has been really, really helping my sense of self. It’s been doing wonders for my anxiety. I’m not even a little anxious about the back door being open right now, I’m just watching and listening to the storm. The raindrops are fat, satisfying, warm. I love summer rain.

Anyway, this new medication. It feels like when you go to the optometrist, and they do the thing where they’re like “one, or two”, and they hit two, and everything snaps into a really sharp, crisp focus. But for my self of self, identity, and inner voice. It’s really pleasant to not be so harshly second-guessing myself, ruminating on things I’ve done or not done, things that have happened to me. I have some sort of ipseity disturbance, and often feel like a collection of 5-100 people more than I feel like one. This medicine has reduced that down to maybe 2-3 different “me”s, and I am pleased to be feeling more integrated.

I am much less pleased by how much energy it seems to be stealing from me. Allegedly, it can take 4-6 weeks to get used to the fatigue and for your body to adjust. This is why I have been having trouble writing, and beyond that, having trouble thinking. It’s nice to feel calmer, less anxious, less pointy, but it’s coming at the cost of sharpness, of ability, of execution. Much like the strawberry plant in on my back porch, I suppose it’s the dose that makes the medicine. Too much rain will drown you. Too much calm will steal my creative spark.

I kind of refuse to idly sit here while it’s stolen from me though. I’ve been pushing fiercely, and I’m determined to post often, even if what I post isn’t very much at all. I’ve been world building, architecting, buying seventy fucking domains on a whim. I hope that what I see, and what I want to make, will enter the world in the way I want it to.

Plans have a nasty habit of not surviving first contact with the enemy.

As such, I have not made a plan. I haven’t even made a roadmap. I’ve barely done the storyboarding. The end is a surprise, mostly because I also don’t know how it ends, so whatever ends up flowing out of me will be at the very least surprising. I’m not good at endings. I prefer the Irish Goodbye, the sort of goodbye that slips you away before anyone notices, and then you sort of muse on what sudden absence feels like.

I’ve had two particularly unsatisfying conflicts resolve this way lately. I don’t mind that these goodbyes were particularly quick and unspoken. The other parties made their own stories up, mythologized, assigned motives, and then swiftly enacted their will. It didn’t actually matter what I had to say, the real Me isn’t as important as the one they made up. I resent the ghosts of these things a lot more than I resent the actions people take once they’ve decided to go this route. It makes me both curious and sad that people don’t really seem to do this, don’t seem interested in finding out if their personalized and constructed inner narratives actually match anything exterior. Maybe they don’t need them to the way I do. Maybe you’re comfortable telling yourself tall tales, comfortable with your campfire myths and legends about the evil people, comfortable in your stagnation. Maybe you don’t ever want to confront that maybe it was more complicated than your myths. Maybe it’s load bearing that you don’t.

I wish I worked that way, but I am far too concerned about how often my insides don’t match my outsides, which I guess is solved in my case by medication, and in regular people it’s just something they never even consider.

Early on in my career in brokerage, I was introduced by some well-meaning managers to the idea of a CLM, or a Career Limiting Move.

A Career Limiting Move is an action you take, that while it might feel extremely good to do — hell, you might even be justified in doing it — will ultimately limit your ability to advance in your career.

I found the idea myopic at the time. While I still think that on a grand scale, it’s something that doesn’t really exist, I do understand that within my local minima of reality there are choices I can make that will cause damage to myself and others, and not allow me to progress forward without great frustration, effort, and broken stuff behind me.

I have been working on my impulse control for years. I am diagnosed by the NCAA as severely ADHD. Literally can’t do it.

To expand on what it means to be called severely ADHD by the NCAA: they put me through rigorous and extensive testing to make sure I could have these sometimes performance enhancing drugs.

For one part of this examination, I was put in a room with no clock. They allegedly forgot to set the timer on the ensuing series of tests. I am pretty sure that is part of the test.

One of the tests the NCAA administers takes place on a computer. You are presented with a black screen. Every second or so, a white letter will appear. Your job is to press the spacebar when you see a letter, any letter, except for the letter X.

During this test, I:

  • Asked how much time had elapsed twelve times
  • Took my shoes off
  • Put them back on
  • Hummed twelve different songs
  • Asked how long it had been another four times
  • Changed position in the chair somewhere between twenty and thirty times
  • Hit the X an astounding 97% of the time (that is, I accidentally hit the spacebar 32 of 33 times an X appeared on the screen)

It was so very obvious that this test was a formality that the woman administering it laughed when I was done. She looked at a printout for all of maybe nine seconds, and said that I’d have my results very shortly.

It’s even easier IRL than it is digitally. People have posting quirks and soft spots for their online friends and are easily found. The natural thing to do here is to segue into an actual report. I’ll start by sort of not starting there.

Once, a dear sweet friend introduced me to someone new by casually remarked that I always have at least one evil eye on me, somewhere, at all times. It is true, I just never realized anyone else noticed.

I have this weird half-baked theory that evil eyes don’t just ward off evil spirits and nefarious energy, they capture and store it. When one of mine breaks or goes missing, it’s because the eye was holding all of the Bad Stuff that it could, but now it’s full and can no longer shield me. This is equal parts superstition and cope for constantly losing my things, but it makes me feel protected.

Tonight I have three and a half evil eye rings on (one is just a bunch of eye-shaped things but the vibe is right so it counts as half), a pair very dangly earrings with evil eyes on them, and a necklace made up of evil eyes. I need all the help I can get.

Yesterday I had some neurosis, and realized I’d gained 10lb since my wedding. In a vaguely-self-harm shaped fit, I upped my dose of tirzepatide to something slightly higher than responsible. I felt OK yesterday, so I think I’ve actually managed to get away with it.

I have many calls, including a really good conversation with my not-Boss, and then another really great conversation with someone that knows said not-Boss. Immediately after the second call ends, my stomach does something terrible.

I end up laying first on the bathroom floor for a while. This turns into me laying down in the shower and not quite wishing for death, but something close. Eventually, I pick myself up and make it outside to my hammock. I can’t really feel the heat, and I stay there, enjoying barely existing.

I order a drink with fish sauce in it. Actually, I ordered three different drinks, because I also wanted a Diet Coke and some iced black mango tea. My friend laughs at this, but then joins me in ordering two different drinks. We cheers to the abundance mindset.

“Heat.
Heat. This is what cities mean to me. You get off the train and walk out of the station and you are hit with the full blast. The heat of air, traffic and people. The heat of food and sex. The heat of tall buildings. The heat that flows out of the subways and tunnels. It's always fifteen degrees hotter in the cities. Heat rises from the sidewalks and falls from the poisoned sky. The buses breathe heat. Heat emanates from crowds of shoppers and office workers, the entire infrastructure is based on heat, desperately uses up heat, breeds more heat. The eventual heat death of the universe that scientists love to talk about is already well underway and you can feel it happening all around you in any large or medium-sized city. Heat and wetness.”

I slip into something more comfortable, shedding my skin and putting on my digital formless shape, and check in on the various group chat ecosystems. I notice tactical retreats by various parties, and roll a lavender-chamomile-mint joint while consider my next move.

A beautiful woman texts me, telling me she’ll be back in New York soon and suggests a rendezvous. I chuckle while lighting my joint (inside, like a heathen) and hit post.
